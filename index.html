"The details are not the details. They make the design."

— Charles Eames

I'm a Mechanical Engineering student at UT Austin with a minor in Semiconductor Engineering and Science, driven by a simple conviction: the best engineering work happens where physical intuition meets computational rigor.

My path has been deliberately broad. At Boeing, I worked on factory floors diagnosing structural defects in 737 airframe assemblies — real hardware, real consequences, real deadlines. In the lab, I spent 1.5 years running 200+ experiments on a novel nanomanufacturing technique (OBEL), achieving sub-micron feature resolution (~450 nm) at ultralow etchant concentrations.

On UT Austin's inaugural Baja SAE team, I designed and welded a competition chassis from scratch — 12 full design iterations, ANSYS and MATLAB FEA, and a final chassis ~50% smaller than the initial concept while meeting FoS ≥ 2. I've also published two peer-reviewed papers and co-presented at an international SPIE conference.

Long-term, I want to design hardware at the intersection of aerospace and precision manufacturing — systems where physics sets the rules and engineering creativity finds the margin.
