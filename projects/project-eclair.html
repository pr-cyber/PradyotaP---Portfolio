ECLAIR Combat
Robotics — Drum Spinner

Designed and manufactured a competitive combat robot featuring a high-kinetic-energy drum-spinner weapon for the ECLAIR Battle Bots competition. The project centered on three engineering objectives: maximizing the kinetic energy stored in the rotating drum at competition weight limits, integrating all drive and weapon electronics inside a chassis designed to survive high-impact collisions, and optimizing component placement to protect sensitive electronics from shock and debris.

The Problem

Combat robotics imposes a unique multi-objective design challenge: the robot must simultaneously deliver maximum kinetic energy to an opponent on offense, survive high-impact collisions on defense, protect sensitive electronic components inside the chassis, and stay within strict competition weight limits — all in the smallest possible physical envelope.

Drum-spinner weapons amplify these tensions. A heavier drum stores more kinetic energy but reduces chassis mass budget for armor and structure. Higher RPM increases impact energy but introduces gyroscopic effects that impair maneuverability. Weapon housing must transmit impact loads without deforming into the drivetrain or electronics bay.

The design strategy: prioritize weapon energy density over chassis weight, accept aggressive weight limits on armor, and compensate through intelligent internal geometry that positions drivetrain and electronics in naturally protected locations relative to anticipated impact vectors.

Design Philosophy

"Design for survival under worst-case impact — not average impact. Position every critical component assuming the worst hit comes from the most exposed direction."

Design Process

1

Weapon Energy Optimization

Calculated kinetic energy stored in the drum as a function of geometry, material, and RPM under competition weight constraints. Optimized drum mass distribution to maximize moment of inertia at the outer radius — concentrating mass at the impact teeth — for maximum energy transfer upon contact. Balanced weapon energy budget against chassis mass requirements.

2

Drum Geometry & Gyroscopic Analysis

Engineered the drum geometry for maximum energy-per-unit-mass while minimizing gyroscopic precession effects during maneuvers. Evaluated angular momentum magnitude and direction to predict handling characteristics during competition driving. Designed the drum bearing mounts to handle combined radial and axial loads from both spinning inertia and impact events.

3

Chassis Design in OnShape

Designed the full chassis assembly in OnShape for cloud-based collaborative modeling, enabling precise tolerances for weapon housing and drive assembly interfaces. Optimized component placement to position ESCs and receivers in structurally shielded locations behind the drivetrain assembly, protected from both frontal impacts and debris ejection from the weapon zone.

4

Drive System & Electronics Integration

Selected and integrated the drive motor, weapon motor, ESCs, and receiver within the chassis constraints. Routed motor leads to minimize debris exposure and designed cable management to prevent cable entanglement during impact events. Designed weapon motor mounting for easy field replacement between rounds.

5

Fabrication & Competition Validation

Manufactured chassis components using available fabrication equipment. Assembled and tested the complete robot through multiple test runs — validating weapon spin-up time, drivetrain response, and gyroscopic handling behavior. Competed in the ECLAIR Battle Bots tournament, validating drive system durability and weapon efficacy under real competition conditions.

Results

⚡

High-RPM drum weapon successfully spun up to operating speed within competition time limits and delivered measurable kinetic energy transfer on contact in competition matches.

🛡

Drive system survived competition impact loading — the electronics shielding geometry kept ESCs and receivers intact through multiple high-energy collision events that would have destroyed less intentionally placed components.

🔍

Key learning — gyroscopic effects are a first-order concern at competition RPMs. Handling behavior was noticeably affected by precession during sharp turns, informing future drum axis orientation and mass distribution decisions.

🤝

OnShape cloud CAD enabled real-time team collaboration — multiple contributors could work on chassis sub-assemblies simultaneously with live interference checking across the full assembly model.
