Mission Challenge

Designing a Human Landing System for NASA's Artemis program is one of the most constraints-intensive engineering problems in aerospace: the vehicle must descend from lunar orbit to the surface safely, support crew surface operations, and re-ascend to rendezvous with the Lunar Gateway — all in a lunar environment with no atmosphere, extreme thermal cycling, micrometeorite exposure, and communications latency that demands onboard autonomy for critical phases.

As Team Lead for Team Delta, the objective was to conduct a rigorous preliminary design study: research all mission-critical subsystems through NASA resources and technical literature, synthesize a coherent systems-level architecture, and develop a detailed prototype model with annotated engineering drawings — suitable for a formal Preliminary Design Review (PDR).

The scope covered crew descent and ascent operations, lunar surface access systems, life support, propulsion, GN&C, communications, power, and radiation protection — requiring synthesis across nine interdependent engineering disciplines.

Scope

"Nine subsystems. One vehicle. A crew's life depending on every interface. Systems engineering means understanding how all of them fail together — not just separately."

02

Design & Research Process

1

Mission Analysis & Requirements Definition

Conducted extensive mission analysis through structured research activities using NASA mission resource repositories, technical databases, and consultant briefings. Synthesized Artemis mission parameters — orbital insertion conditions, lunar surface access geometries, crew habitation requirements, and Lunar Gateway staging procedures — into a formal requirements set for the HLS design.

2

Landing Site Analysis & Surface Operations Planning

Evaluated candidate lunar landing sites based on scientific value, operational feasibility, communication line-of-sight to Earth and Lunar Gateway, and solar illumination for power generation. Analyzed surface terrain hazard avoidance requirements and developed crew surface transit corridor geometries relative to the landing footpad location.

3

Systems Architecture — Nine Subsystem Integration

Developed integrated systems architecture spanning: ECLSS (life support), propulsion system, Guidance Navigation & Control (GN&C) with precision landing and hazard avoidance, LunaNet communication architecture, electrical power system, radiation shielding, docking interface for Lunar Gateway, crew egress/ingress system, and structural load path.

4

Cutaway Prototype Development

Engineered a sectional cutaway model revealing internal systems layout and component integration. Developed dimensional specifications for all major subsystems, annotated with engineering rationale for geometric and mass trade-offs. The cutaway model served as the primary visual communication tool for the PDR presentation, making subsystem interactions and interface logic legible to a technical review audience.

5

PDR Documentation & Presentation

Produced comprehensive technical documentation including: mission analysis summary, subsystem design rationale reports, engineering trade studies, materials and mass budget, and a full PDR presentation synthesizing requirements, design solutions, and system capabilities. Managed team roles, resource allocation, and review cycles to meet all PDR deliverable deadlines.
