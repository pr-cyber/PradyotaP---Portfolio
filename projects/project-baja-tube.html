Baja SAE Chassis
Tube Optimization

Developed an end-to-end analytical and computational framework in MATLAB to optimize a Baja SAE tubular chassis for strength, stiffness, weight, and cost. The tool performs a full parametric sweep of tube outer diameter and wall thickness across multiple steel alloys — evaluating bending stiffness, Euler buckling, torsional rigidity, mass, and material cost — then runs a custom 3D space-frame FEA model with color-mapped deformation visualization to validate Pareto-optimal design candidates.

The Problem

For a Baja SAE chassis, tube selection is one of the highest-leverage design decisions: the same geometry enforced by the SAE rulebook must survive frontal impacts, rollover loads, and torsional twist — while staying as light and inexpensive as possible. No single tube specification optimizes all four dimensions simultaneously.

Manual selection from material datasheets produces locally reasonable choices but misses the interaction effects between diameter, wall thickness, alloy choice, and load case. A team starting from scratch also has no institutional knowledge to draw on, making a principled, data-driven selection method essential.

The goal: build a MATLAB framework that automates the full analytical sweep, filters by safety-factor constraints, identifies the Pareto front across mass and cost, and validates finalist candidates with 3D beam FEA — all exportable to CSV for downstream documentation.

Methodology

1

Full Parametric Sweep — Geometry × Material

Swept tube outer diameter (1.0″–2.5″) and wall thickness (0.049″–0.134″) across AISI 1018, AISI 4130, and Docol R8 steel. For each combination, computed bending stiffness (EI), bending strength, Euler buckling load, torsional rigidity (GJ), section mass per meter, total chassis mass, and total material cost.

2

Safety-Factor Filtering

Applied rule-based thresholds enforcing minimum bending stiffness (Kb ≥ 2620 N·m²), minimum bending strength (Sb ≥ 374 N·m), safety factor ≥ 1.5, and a maximum wall-to-diameter ratio of 0.15. Filtered the full candidate set down to only rule-compliant configurations before Pareto analysis.

3

Pareto Front Identification (Mass vs. Cost)

Computed the Pareto-optimal front across total chassis mass and total material cost for each alloy. Pareto candidates represent designs where no other option is better on both metrics simultaneously — the engineering-efficient boundary of the feasible design space. Up to 6 Pareto candidates per alloy were selected for FEA validation.

4

3D Space-Frame FEA — Euler–Bernoulli Beam Elements

Built a custom 3D beam FEA solver in MATLAB using 12-DOF Euler–Bernoulli frame elements. Chassis node coordinates were approximated from the SolidWorks CAD model. Applied representative combined loading: driver weight (800 N distributed across seat nodes) and lateral roll load (2,500 N at roll-hoop nodes). Solved for nodal displacements and computed element midpoint displacement magnitudes.

5

Color-Mapped Deformation Overlay Visualization

Plotted undeformed chassis geometry (black wireframe) overlaid with deformed configurations for all Pareto candidates, color-mapped by element displacement magnitude using the jet colormap. Scale factor set so maximum displacement corresponds to ~8% of chassis diagonal for readability. Results exported to CSV for downstream design review and manufacturing documentation.

Key Results & Insights

📐

1,000+ tube configurations evaluated in seconds vs. days of manual calculation — covering the full feasible design space across three alloys.

📊

Pareto-optimal candidates identified per alloy: AISI 4130 dominated the mass-cost frontier at mid-range wall thicknesses. Docol R8's high yield strength enabled thinner walls but at significantly higher material cost and lower weldability.

🔍

FEA revealed load path asymmetry not visible in the 1D analytic sweep: the rear roll-hoop nodes carried disproportionate displacement under combined loading, informing targeted reinforcement decisions in the physical chassis design.

📋

Results exported to CSV for team documentation, including OD, wall thickness, total mass, total cost, safety factors, and predicted max displacement per candidate.

🔗

Framework is fully reusable: swap in new node coordinates from updated CAD models, change material properties, or adjust load cases — the entire sweep and visualization regenerates automatically.
